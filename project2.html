Boccia Physics

Play boccia with real world physics on your browser.

What is Boccia Physics?

Boccia Physics is an engaging browser game that emulates the traditional game of boccia using real-world physics. Players aim to get their balls as close to a hole as possible, but instead of throwing, the balls are propelled off a ramp. The game realistically simulates various physical forces, including gravity, wind, and the force of the spring. Players must strategically adjust the angle of the ramp and consider multiple factors to achieve the best results. Test your skill and precision in this funny little game that brings boccia to the digital realm.

The game was developed as part of a semester course, focusing on the accurate depiction of physics rather than detailed graphics. The primary objective was to emulate the game of boccia within a browser environment, utilizing real-world physics to determine the movement and behavior of the balls. This emphasis on physics over graphics allows for a highly accurate and educational representation of the game.

Project Overview

Boccia Physics was predominantly developed using JavaScript, leveraging the p5.js library to establish foundational graphics. The project evolved through multiple stages, each dedicated to integrating different principles of physics. The primary challenge lay in translating intricate physical formulas into a digital framework, ensuring accurate simulation and realistic gameplay. My responsibilities included, but were not limited to:

  • Creating the playfield
  • Implementing interactive elements
  • Handling collisions
  • Applying gravity
  • Simulating wind forces
  • Incorporating drag (air and ground resistance)
  • Applying Newtonian forces
